Most of the time a sauna is used, only part of it is spent in the heat. The rest
is the cooling — standing outside, sitting down, letting the temperature come
back. A cabin format makes room for that.

Splitting the footprint into a hot room and a covered outdoor area means the
whole cycle happens in one place. You are not walking wet through the house
between rounds, and in cold or wet weather the covered section is what makes the
sauna usable year-round rather than seasonally.

It asks for more ground than a barrel and a more considered approach to siting:
drainage, a level base, and a route to it that works in the dark.

The cube is the most architectural of the sauna forms. Where a barrel announces
itself, a cube sits quietly — a rectilinear volume that reads as a small building
rather than an object dropped in the garden.

The format's real argument is the window. A flat roof and square walls allow
glazing across an entire elevation, which changes what the room is for: you sit
facing outward, and the landscape becomes part of the ritual rather than
something you left behind at the door.

Bench layouts generally run along two facing walls, with the upper tier set at
the height where the heat gathers. Placement matters more than with other forms —
a cube wants a considered spot, an approach, and something worth looking at.

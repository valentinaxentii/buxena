A barrel is a piece of engineering as much as a piece of joinery. Curving the
walls removes the cold corners of a square room and cuts the air volume that has
to be brought up to temperature, which is why barrels have a reputation for
heating quickly.

The staves are drawn together under tension by steel bands, so the structure
holds itself. Rain runs off the curve rather than sitting on it. There is very
little to go wrong, which is part of the appeal.

Inside, the geometry does something particular: the ceiling curves down to meet
the bench, so the heat sits close around you rather than pooling in a void
overhead. It is a smaller, more enclosed experience than a cabin — closer to the
older tradition.
